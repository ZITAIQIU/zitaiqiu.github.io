AWARDS

Customers can drive the lunar rover to explore the lunar surface according to the route designed by the hotel and understand the physical phenomena on the lunar surface.

  • Tycho Crater

    "Tycho appears as a bright spot in the southern highlands with rays of bright material that stretch across much of the nearside. Its prominence is not due to its size: at 85 km in diameter, it's just one among thousands of this size or larger. What makes Tycho stand out is its relative youth." ("Tycho Crater", n.d.)

  • Mare Orientale

    "The Orientale Basin is located near the western limb of the lunar nearside and is only partially visible from telescopes on Earth. Lunar Orbiter images provided the first good look at this basin, which is 930 kilometers in diameter." ("Mare Orientale", n.d.)

  • Linné Crater

    "Linné crater is a beautifully preserved young Moon crater. The 1.4 mile (2.2 kilometer) crater is is thought to be less than 10 million years old. Craters don’t erode nearly as quickly on the Moon, but Linné is pristine even for a lunar crater; it shows no signs of any subsequent major impacts, retaining its original shape." ("Linné Crater", n.d.)

  • South Pole-Aitken Basin

    "The South Pole-Aitken basin is the largest and oldest recognized impact basin on the Moon. It's diameter is roughly 1,550 miles (2,500 kilometers). The Moon's circumference is just under 6,800 miles (11,000 kilometers), meaning the basin stretches across nearly a quarter of the Moon." ("South Pole-Aitken Basin", n.d.)

  • Van De Graaf Crater

    "Unlike other merged craters on the Moon, there is no rim separating the two sections of this crater. A relatively strong magnetic field was detected near this crater. This discovery is unusual because the Moon does not currently have a global magnetic field like the Earth does." ("Van De Graaf Crater", n.d.)

  • Stratton

    "UA high concentration of iron makes this area on the Moon's far side a point of scientific interest. It is named for British astrophysicist Frederick J.M. Stratton (1881-1960)." ("Stratton", n.d.)
